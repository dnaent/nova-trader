4. **Blockchain *infrastructure*** (BCHN.L +55%, BCHS.L +58%) — note: *infrastructure ETFs* won, pure crypto-sentiment proxies lost (see below).

### Characteristics of WINNERS
- **Profitable, revenue-generating leaders** in the AI buildout ("picks and shovels") — foundries, memory, power, networking — not speculative end-applications.
- **Commodity producers with real output** supplying supply-constrained inputs (copper, gold, lithium, rare earth, uranium).
- **Diversified thematic ETFs** capturing a whole trend (semis, AI, robotics) rather than single-stock lottery tickets.

### Characteristics of LOSERS (negative training examples)
- **Pre-revenue / early-stage story-stocks** whose deployment is years off: SMR (−70%, small modular reactors), TMC (−24%, deep-sea mining pre-production).
- **Leveraged / treasury sentiment proxies:** MSTR (−69%), COIN (−45%) — high beta to a single narrative.
- **Rate-sensitive yield vehicles in a high-rate regime:** NESF.L (−34%, solar), UKW.L (−10%, wind) — income trusts de-rate as rates rise.
- **Round-tripped hype:** CRWV (−31%).

### The rule of thumb for the model
> Favour **profitable leaders and real producers in supply-constrained inputs to a growing demand wave** (today: AI compute + its chips/power/metals). Be cautious on **pre-revenue story-stocks, leveraged single-narrative proxies, and rate-sensitive yield when rates are high.** Prefer **diversified thematic ETFs** over single-name lottery bets for core exposure. A defensive anchor (gold, global, income) is what lets a growth book survive a crash without a lost decade.
